About Moodle LMS

  • Modular Object-Oriented Dynamic Learning Environment (MOODLE)
  • Helps Educator to create personalised learning environments using Moodle Activities and Resources.
  • Approx. 213 million users worldwide. Recently MoodleUS was lauched. Source: https://stats.moodle.org/
  • Moodle delivers a powerful set of learner-centric tools and collaborative learning environments that empower both teaching and learning. 
  • Design and development of Moodle is guided by a “social constructionist pedagogy”.
  • Free with no licensing fees - Moodle is provided freely as Open Source software, under the GNU General Public License. 

Formative Assessment/Evaluation

  • The concept of “formative evaluation” was introduced by Scriven (1967) - ‘Scriven, M. (1967). The Methodology of Evaluation. In R. W. Tyler, R. M. Gagne, & M. Scriven (Eds.), Perspectives of Curriculum Evaluation Chicago (pp. 39-83)’

Formative VS Summative assessment

Formative Summative
In-class discussions Instructor-created exams
Clicker questions Standardized tests
Low-stakes group work Final projects
Weekly quizzes Final essays
1-minute reflection writing assignments Final presentations
Homework assignments Final reports

Good feedback practice:

  • helps clarify what good performance is (goals, criteria, expected standards);
  • facilitates the development of self-assessment (reflection) in learning;
  • delivers high quality information to students about their learning;
  • encourages teacher and peer dialogue around learning;
  • encourages positive motivational beliefs and self-esteem;
  • provides opportunities to close the gap between current and desired performance;
  • provides information to teachers that can be used to help shape teaching.

Formative assessment of student Projects using Moodle Assignment activity

  • Rubrics based evaluation: Sample activity: https://moovit.vit.ac.in/grade/grading/manage.php?areaid=2906
  • Students create group as a activity (Using Moodle Group Self-Selection activity).
  • Students choose time slot for review as specified by Faculty. (Moodle Scheduler plugin).
  • Feedback to entire group and reopening submission if required.
  • Link based submissions for Project demo with audio explanation. Sample activity: https://moovit.vit.ac.in/mod/assign/view.php?id=28808&rownum=0&action=grader&userid=93687

Encourage and facilitate online discussions with Moodle forums

  • The Forum activity allows students and teachers to exchange ideas by posting comments as part of a ’thread'.
  • Sample activity: https://moovit.vit.ac.in/mod/forum/view.php?id=22116

Peer learning using Moodle Database plugin

  • The Database activity allows the teacher and/or students to build, display and search a bank of record entries about any conceivable topic.
  • Sample Activity: https://moovit.vit.ac.in/mod/data/view.php?id=28033

Engage Learners with Gamification in Moodle

  • Develop learning pathways - incentivise your learners to follow pre-defined learning routes to attain the knowledge or skills

  • Sample Activity: https://moovit.vit.ac.in/blocks/xp/index.php/ladder/10572

Peer assessment using workshop activity

  • Workshop is a powerful peer assessment activity. Students add submissions which are then distributed amongst their peers for assessment based on a grading scale specified by the teacher.
  • Sample Activity: https://moovit.vit.ac.in/mod/workshop/view.php?id=30099
